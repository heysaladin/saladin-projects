Top Saving Tips Only Known to Banking Experts TRAVEL | DECEMBER 27, 2017

Fundamentals of managing your financial accounts: How do you balance your income and expenses? Growing financial burdens and declining income require you to reconsider your lifestyle and to start planning and managing your financial accounts with greater awareness. It has become imperative that you balance your income and expenditures within a clear and specific budget that allows you not only to define your expenses but also to save for the future.

Determine your income and expenses In order to be able to set a successful and effective budget, you must know exactly the income you get and your expenses. While it may be easy for you to determine your income, expenses have a different story. Many people write down expenses by grouping them under a variety of expenses classification, but this method is not right. The best way to track your expenses is by writing down everything you spend within one month on a sheet of paper and then at the end of the month review your expenses and classify them into various categories, such as food, entertainment, shopping, and more. This method also helps you identify and stop unnecessary expenses.

Determine your financial position After this month, you will be able to determine your financial position. Are your expenses more than your income, so you are in trouble and you must start immediately to reduce your expenses, but if what you spend is less than your income, then this means that you are on the right path. To be able to reduce your expenses, you must first separate the necessary expenses such as electricity and telephone bills, and then calculate the rest. The rest of the money can be distributed over your other expenses

How to save After calculating the necessary expenses, you must allocate between 10 percent and 25 percent of the remaining money to save for the future, thereby ensuring that a sum of money is available for emergency situations. The remaining amount can be distributed among your other luxury purchases

Income assessment Try to live during the month within your budget. At the same time, you can reconsider your income, and think about available ways to either cut costs or increase income. You can reduce necessary expenses such as moving to a less expensive home or reducing electricity consumption at home. You can also diversify your source of income through various investments

Planning ahead You should set a budget for the month as well as for the future. The future budget will include additional expenses such as holidays or other. As it is necessary to allocate a budget for these expenditures ahead of time, and saving to achieve them. You should also consider contingencies, and if they do occur, they can disrupt your budget. Think about possible expenditures over the next year, and assign a specific category to them

If you follow these steps, you will be able to set a budget and stick to it, and always remember that the budget is subject to change, do not expect it to remain the same forever. Review your budget every 6 months and see what needs to be adjusted
